---
title: "Why Reply Rates Vary"
---

"Why is my reply rate 12% when you said 20% is possible?" Fair question. Reply rates depend on several factors, most of which have nothing to do with the quality of our work.

## Industry Matters (A Lot)

Some industries are easier to reach on LinkedIn than others. Based on data from 20M+ outreach attempts across the industry:

| Prospect's Industry | Avg Reply Rate |
|---------------------|---------------|
| Legal/Professional Services | 10.4% |
| Healthcare | 9.3% |
| Retail/Consumer Goods | 9.2% |
| Education | ~8% |
| Marketing | ~7% |
| Construction | ~7% |
| Software/SaaS | 4.8% |

Software/SaaS prospects get bombarded with outreach. Their inboxes are noisy, so reply rates are naturally lower. That doesn't mean campaigns targeting SaaS buyers can't work. It means a 12% reply rate in SaaS is actually excellent, even though it would be average in healthcare.

**For context:** Our portfolio average across all clients is 28.1% reply rate, well above every industry baseline above.

## Seniority Affects Response

| Prospect's Role | Avg Reply Rate |
|-----------------|---------------|
| HR/Talent Acquisition | 12.1% |
| Product | 10.2% |
| Operations | 10.0% |
| C-level | 7.0% |
| VP/Director | 7.0% |
| Marketing | 6.4% |
| Sales | 6.3% |

C-suite and VP-level prospects reply less frequently, but when they do, those conversations tend to be higher quality. A 7% reply rate from CEOs is worth more than a 15% reply rate from junior managers.

## Other Factors

**Company size:** Mid-market (50-500 employees) tends to respond at higher rates than enterprise (1000+). Enterprise prospects have more gatekeepers and more competing outreach.

**Messaging quality:** This is the one we can control. Good copy (short, specific, relevant) consistently outperforms generic templates. That's why we A/B test and refresh messaging regularly.

**Timing and seasonality:** December and August are traditionally slower months. Q1 and Q4 tend to be stronger because budget decisions are being made.

**List quality:** A targeted list of 500 perfect-fit prospects will outperform a broad list of 5,000 every time. Quality over quantity.

## The Takeaway

Don't compare your reply rate to another company in a completely different industry selling to a completely different buyer. Compare it to where you were last month. That's the trend that matters.
