---
title: "Campaign Health Scores Explained"
---

We score every campaign on a 0-100 scale so you can see at a glance how things are performing. Here's what the scores mean.

## The Scale

| Score | Rating | What it means |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| 80-100 | Top Performer | Exceeding benchmarks. We're looking at how to scale this. |
| 60-79 | Healthy | Meeting expectations. We may run small tests for improvement. |
| 40-59 | Needs Attention | Below benchmark on at least one metric. We're actively optimizing. |
| 0-39 | At Risk | Underperforming. We're making changes and will walk you through the plan. |

## What Goes Into the Score

The health score is based on a few key inputs:

- **Reply rate** is the biggest factor. A campaign with a strong reply rate will always score well.
- **Connection rate** matters too. If people aren't accepting connections, the rest of the funnel can't work.
- **Reply quality** counts. A campaign with a 15% reply rate where half the replies are "not interested" scores lower than one with a 12% reply rate where most replies are genuinely curious.

## What the Score Doesn't Tell You

The health score is a snapshot. It doesn't capture everything. A campaign could score 55 but have 3 hot leads in active conversation. Or it could score 75 but all the replies are from the wrong seniority level.

That's why we pair the score with commentary in your weekly update. The number is a starting point, not the whole story.

## When Should You Worry?

If a campaign drops below 40, we'll flag it proactively and outline what we're doing about it. You don't need to monitor scores constantly. That's our job. But if you ever want to check in, the score gives you a quick read.

## Score Trends Matter More Than Single Scores

A campaign that's been at 65 for 6 weeks is performing consistently. A campaign that dropped from 72 to 48 in two weeks tells us something changed. We track trends and react before scores fall off a cliff.
