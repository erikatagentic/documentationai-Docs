title: "When to Expect Results"
---

One of the most common questions we get. Here's the honest answer, with real timelines.

## The Short Version

- First connections accepted: Days 1-3 after launch
- First replies: Days 3-7
- First real conversations: Week 2
- First meetings booked: Weeks 2-4 (but this varies a lot)
- Steady, predictable performance: Week 6-8
- Full ramp: 90 days

## Why It Takes Time

LinkedIn outreach isn't like turning on a faucet. When you send a connection request, the recipient might not check LinkedIn for a few days. Once they accept, your first follow-up message goes out 1-2 days later. They might not reply for another few days.

So from the moment a connection request goes out to the moment you could have a reply, you're looking at 5-10 days minimum. And that's just one person. You need volume for patterns to emerge.

## The 30-Day Trap

Most clients who get frustrated do so around Day 21-28. They've been live for three weeks, replies are just starting to come in, and it doesn't feel like enough yet. This is the hardest part because the data is too small to be statistically meaningful but too present to ignore.

Our advice: look at trends, not individual days. Are connections being accepted? Are people opening your messages? Those are leading indicators that the pipeline is building.

## What "Results" Actually Look Like

Results don't always show up as a booked meeting on Day 14. More often it looks like:

- A VP replies saying "Interesting, tell me more"
- Someone asks for a case study
- A prospect says "Not now, but reach out in Q3"
- A referral: "Talk to my colleague about this"

All of these are pipeline. They may not convert this week, but they're conversations that wouldn't have happened without the campaign.

## When to Be Concerned

If you're 30+ days in and seeing zero replies across all campaigns, something needs to change. That's not normal and we won't wait around. We'd already be testing new messaging, adjusting targeting, or recommending a different approach.
