---
title: "How A/B Testing Works"
---

We don't guess what messaging will work. We test it. Here's how our A/B testing process works and what to expect.

## What We Test

The most common things we test:
- **Messaging angles:** Different ways to frame your value proposition (cost savings vs. speed vs. outcomes)
- **CTAs:** "Worth a quick call?" vs. "Want me to send over a case study?" vs. "How are you handling X today?"
- **Follow-up timing:** 2-day gaps vs. 5-day gaps between messages
- **Connection notes:** Blank vs. short signal-based note
- **Opening lines:** Different hooks in the first follow-up message

## How We Test

One variable at a time. If we change the opening line AND the CTA AND the timing all at once, we can't tell what actually moved the needle. So we isolate one change, run it, and compare.

Each variant needs at least 50 messages sent before we can judge it. With smaller samples, random variation makes the data unreliable. Most tests run for 7-14 days depending on your daily volume.

## How You'll See Results

Your weekly update includes test results when a test has enough data to call. We'll show you:
- What we tested (old copy vs. new copy)
- The reply rate for each variant
- Our recommendation (keep the winner, test something new, or run it longer)

## What We Don't Do

We don't test everything at once, and we don't change what's already working. If a campaign is performing above benchmark, we leave it alone and focus testing on the underperforming campaigns.

We also don't run tests with tiny samples. If your volume is low (under 50 messages per week), we'll focus on one strong approach instead of splitting an already small audience.

## Timeline

Expect the first 30 days to establish baseline performance. Testing usually starts in the second month, once we have enough data to know what's working and what isn't. From there, it's a continuous cycle: test, learn, apply, repeat.
