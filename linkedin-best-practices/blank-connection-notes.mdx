---
title: "Why Connection Notes Are Usually Blank"
---

One of the most common questions we get: "Shouldn't we include a message with the connection request?" The short answer is usually no. Here's why.

## The Data

Across thousands of connection requests, blank requests and personalized notes accept at nearly identical rates. In one study of 20 million+ outreach attempts, blank notes got a 26.37% acceptance rate. Personalized notes got 26.42%. Statistically, no difference.

But here's where it gets interesting: connection requests that include a pitch in the note consistently underperform. Acceptance rates drop to 14-25% when the note sounds like a sales message. People see the pitch before they even accept, and they pass.

## When Blank Works Best

Blank connection requests work well when:
- Your LinkedIn profile headline clearly signals value to the prospect
- The prospect can see why you'd want to connect (same industry, mutual connections, relevant role)
- You're targeting people who accept requests from professionals in their space

Most of our campaigns default to blank requests for this reason.

## When a Note Helps

A short note (under 15 words) can outperform blank when there's a specific, real reason to connect:
- You just attended the same conference
- You commented on their recent LinkedIn post
- You have a mutual connection who referred you
- You're in the same alumni network or professional group

The key: the note has to be about them, not about you. "Saw your talk at SaaStr, really liked your point about PLG pricing" works. "I'd love to connect and tell you about our platform" doesn't.

## Why We Default to Blank

For most campaigns, blank requests are the safest and most effective default. They avoid the risk of a note that sounds templated (which hurts more than it helps), and they let your profile do the talking. If we see an opportunity where a signal-based note would work, we'll test it.
