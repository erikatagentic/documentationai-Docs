---
title: "How We Protect Your LinkedIn Account"
---

Your LinkedIn account is your professional identity. We take its safety seriously. Here's what we do behind the scenes to keep it protected.

## Dedicated Browser Profiles

Every LinkedIn sender account gets its own dedicated browser profile. This means your account always logs in from the same virtual environment with the same browser fingerprint. LinkedIn sees consistent behavior, which is exactly what a real user looks like.

## Residential Proxies

Each account connects through a dedicated residential proxy. In plain terms: we make it look like your LinkedIn sessions are always coming from the same location. No IP hopping, no VPN-style red flags. Just steady, consistent access from one place.

## Conservative Daily Limits

We don't push volume to the edge. Most accounts run at 25-35 connection requests per day, well within LinkedIn's informal comfort zone. New accounts start even lower (15-20/day) and ramp up over 2-3 weeks. See "LinkedIn Daily Limits and Account Warmup" for the full breakdown.

## Warmup Before Launch

Before any campaign goes live, your sender account goes through a warmup period. We gradually increase activity over the first few weeks so LinkedIn's algorithms see natural growth, not a sudden spike.

## Daily Monitoring

We check account health every day. If LinkedIn starts throttling your requests or shows signs of unusual activity detection, we pull back volume before it becomes a restriction. In most cases, we catch issues before you'd ever notice them.

## What Happens If Something Goes Wrong

If your account does get temporarily restricted (it happens occasionally, even with precautions), we pause all campaigns immediately, reduce limits, and wait for the restriction to lift. Most restrictions clear within 24-72 hours. We don't flip everything back on at once. We ramp back gradually to avoid triggering a second restriction.

For the full rundown on restrictions, see "My LinkedIn Account Got Restricted" in the Troubleshooting section.
