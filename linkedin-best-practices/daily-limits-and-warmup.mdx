---
title: "LinkedIn Daily Limits and Account Warmup"
---

LinkedIn doesn't publish official limits on connection requests, but there are informal thresholds that matter. Going over them is one of the fastest ways to get your account restricted. Here's how it works and why we start slow.

## The Daily Limit

Most LinkedIn accounts can safely send 30-40 connection requests per day without triggering any flags. Go above that on a newer or smaller account and you risk a temporary restriction. Older accounts with large networks can sometimes handle more, but we don't push it.

## Why New Accounts Need Warmup

If your LinkedIn profile hasn't been used for outreach before, we don't start at full volume. We begin at 15-20 connection requests per day and increase gradually over 2-3 weeks. This mirrors natural LinkedIn behavior and keeps your account in good standing.

Think of it like a new email address. If you send 500 emails on day one, spam filters catch it. Same concept here.

## What the Warmup Period Looks Like

- **Week 1:** 15-20 connection requests per day
- **Week 2:** 20-25 per day (if account health looks good)
- **Week 3+:** 25-35 per day (steady state for most accounts)

We monitor account health daily during warmup. If LinkedIn shows any signs of throttling, we pull back before it becomes a restriction.

## Why We Don't Just Max It Out

More volume doesn't always mean better results. Accounts running at 35 connections per day with a 25% accept rate will generate more conversations than accounts running at 50 per day with a 12% accept rate (because LinkedIn started throttling them).

The goal is sustainable volume that compounds over weeks, not a sprint that gets your account flagged.

## What This Means for You

Expect campaigns to ramp up gradually in the first 2-3 weeks. The early days will look quiet. That's normal and intentional. Your account manager monitors the limits and adjusts automatically. You don't need to do anything on your end.
