---
title: "How to Respond to Leads"
---

When a prospect replies to your outreach, that conversation is yours. We surface the lead, you close it. Here's how to make the most of each reply.

## Speed Matters

Reply within 24 hours if you can. Within 4 hours is even better. Prospects who get a quick, thoughtful response are significantly more likely to take a meeting. After 72 hours, most opportunities go cold.

## Types of Replies You'll See

**Interested ("Tell me more", "What does this look like?", "Send me info"):**
Don't dump your entire pitch. Ask a qualifying question instead. Something like: "Happy to share more. Quick question first, are you currently doing X in-house or outsourcing it?" This moves the conversation forward without overwhelming them.

**Soft interest ("Maybe after Q2", "Not right now but keep me posted"):**
Acknowledge the timing, don't push. Something like: "Totally get it. I'll circle back in a few months. In the meantime, here's a quick case study that might be relevant when the timing's right." Then actually follow up when they said.

**Negative ("Not interested", "Remove me", "Stop messaging me"):**
Don't reply. We automatically stop outreach to anyone who opts out. No need to apologize or try to save it.

**Questions ("What do you charge?", "How does this work?"):**
Answer directly. Don't redirect to a call if they're asking a simple factual question. Save the call ask for after you've answered their question and they're still engaged.

**Referral ("Talk to my colleague instead"):**
Gold. Thank them, ask for the colleague's name, and reach out mentioning the referral. Referral intros convert at 3-4x the rate of cold outreach.

## What NOT to Do

- Don't copy-paste a long pitch. Keep your first reply under 4 sentences.
- Don't send a calendar link in your first response. Earn the meeting first.
- Don't ignore warm replies for more than 48 hours. That's leaving money on the table.
- Don't apologize for reaching out. You're offering something of value.
