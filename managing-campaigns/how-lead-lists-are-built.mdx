---
title: "How Your Lead List Is Built"
---

Your lead list is the foundation of every campaign. Here's how we build it, how to request changes, and what happens when it runs low.

## How We Build Your Target List

During onboarding, we work with you to define your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP). This includes:
- **Job titles:** Who are you trying to reach? (VP of Sales, CTO, Founder, etc.)
- **Industries:** What sectors are the best fit for your product or service?
- **Company size:** Employee count range that makes sense for your offer (e.g., 50-500 employees)
- **Geography:** Where your customers are located
- **Exclusions:** Companies or industries you don't want to target (competitors, existing clients, specific verticals)

We use these criteria to pull targeted prospect lists from LinkedIn Sales Navigator. Every lead matches the filters you've approved.

## Reviewing Your Leads

You can see your lead lists in the dashboard. If you spot prospects who don't fit, let your account manager know. We'll adjust the filters. Sometimes the fix is small, like tightening the company size range from "10-1000" to "50-500" to cut out companies that are too small.

## Requesting Changes

If your ICP shifts (new market, different buyer persona, updated exclusions), email your account manager. We can adjust targeting criteria and build a fresh list within a few days. Common requests:
- "Exclude anyone in healthcare"
- "Focus only on companies with 100+ employees"
- "Add CFOs to the target list alongside VPs of Finance"

## When a List Runs Low

Lead lists aren't infinite. If a campaign runs long enough, it will exhaust its list (everyone matching your criteria has already been contacted). When this happens, we'll let you know and recommend one of two options:

1. **Expand the criteria** slightly to reach adjacent prospects (e.g., adding a new title or widening the geography)
2. **Build a new list** with a different angle or segment

We monitor list levels weekly so you're never caught off guard.
