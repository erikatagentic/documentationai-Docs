---
title: "How to Review Your Messaging"
---

Before any campaign launches, we send you a messaging document for review. Here's how to read it and give us feedback that actually speeds things up.

## What's in the Messaging Document

Your messaging doc contains the full outreach sequence we'll use in your campaigns:

- **Connection Request:** The note (or lack of note) sent with each connection request. Sometimes we leave this blank on purpose because blank requests often get higher accept rates.
- **Follow-Up 1:** Sent 1-2 days after someone accepts your connection. This is the most important message in the sequence.
- **Follow-Up 2:** Sent 5-7 days after Follow-Up 1. Usually shorter, a nudge or value drop.
- **Follow-Up 3:** Sent 5-7 days after Follow-Up 2. Brief check-in, last touch.
- **InMail:** A standalone message for reaching people you're not connected with.

Each message type has 2 variants (A and B) so we can test what performs better.

## How to Give Useful Feedback

The feedback that helps us most is specific and tied to your business reality.

**Useful feedback:**
- "We actually call this a 'growth audit,' not a 'consultation.' Can you swap that?"
- "Our clients care more about speed to hire than cost savings. Can we lead with that angle?"
- "This stat isn't accurate for our company. Here's the real number."
- "The tone feels too formal for my audience. Can we make it more casual?"

**Feedback that slows things down:**
- "I don't like it" (without saying what or why)
- "Can you make it more professional?" (this usually makes messages sound generic and AI-written)
- "Add more about our services" (longer messages consistently underperform)

## Turnaround

After you send feedback, we typically turn around a revised version within 24-48 hours. One round of revisions is normal. If we go back and forth more than twice, we'll suggest a quick call to align.

## One Thing to Keep in Mind

Short messages outperform long ones on LinkedIn. If a message feels too brief, that's usually a good sign. We test everything, so if a longer version would work better for your audience, the data will show it.
