---
title: "What to Expect in Your First 90 Days"
---

LinkedIn outreach is a compounding channel. The first 30 days are about building data, not booking meetings. Here's what that timeline actually looks like.

## Days 1-14: Setup and Launch

We finalize your ICP targeting (titles, industries, company size, exclusions), build your lead lists, and write your connection request and follow-up sequence. You'll approve the messaging before anything goes live.

Campaigns typically go live by end of week 2.

**What you'll see:** Your campaign status moves to "Active" and the first connection requests start going out.

## Days 15-30: First Data Points

Connections start getting accepted. There's usually a 3-5 day lag between sending a connection request and getting an accept, so don't panic if the first few days look quiet.

Once connections are accepted, your follow-up messages fire automatically. Initial reply data starts trickling in.

**Realistic benchmarks at Day 30:**
- Connection rate: 15-25% is healthy, above 25% is strong
- Reply rate: too early to judge (small sample size)
- Weekly volume: 50-100 connections sent is normal

Meetings at this stage are a bonus, not the norm. If they happen, great. But the data is still building.

## Days 31-60: Optimization Cycle

Now we have enough data to see what's working. We run A/B tests on messaging (one variable at a time), refine your ICP based on who's actually replying, and adjust follow-up copy.

**Realistic benchmarks at Day 60:**
- Connection rate: 15-25% (should be stable by now)
- Reply rate: 10-20% is healthy, above 20% is excellent
- Positive reply ratio: 30-50% of replies show genuine interest

## Days 61-90: Performance Baseline

Campaigns hit steady state. We establish your baseline metrics for ongoing comparison and have a strategic conversation about what to scale, what to pivot, and whether adding profiles or channels makes sense.

This is where ROI math starts to work. Month 1 builds the pipeline. Month 2 optimizes it. Month 3 is where consistent, predictable results show up.

**One thing we've seen over and over:** clients who stay past 90 days almost always renew. The ones who churn usually do it before the system has had time to optimize. Give it the full ramp.
