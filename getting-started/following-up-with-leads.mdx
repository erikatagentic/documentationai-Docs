---
title: "Best Practices for Following Up With Leads"
---

When a prospect replies to your outreach, the automated sequence stops and the conversation is yours. The "How to Respond to Leads" article covers the initial response. This one is about what happens after that, the follow-up cadence that turns a reply into a meeting.

## The Follow-Up Timeline

Not every reply leads to an immediate meeting. Most conversations take 2-4 exchanges before someone agrees to a call. Here's a general cadence that works:

- **First reply from you:** Within 24 hours of their message (4 hours is even better)
- **If they don't respond to your reply:** Follow up 3-5 days later with a different angle or a lighter touch
- **If still no response:** One final follow-up 7-10 days later. Keep it short, 1-2 sentences max
- **After 3 attempts with no response:** Move on. You can revisit in 2-3 months with a fresh reason to reach out

## Keep It Conversational

LinkedIn is a messaging platform, not email. Your follow-ups should feel like a conversation between two professionals, not a sales sequence. A few things that help:

- Ask one question per message (not three)
- Reference something they said in their reply
- Keep messages under 4 sentences
- Don't attach PDFs or long links in the first few exchanges

## Common Mistakes

**Sending a calendar link too early.** Earn the meeting first. Answer their questions, show you understand their situation, then suggest a time. "Would Thursday at 2pm work for a quick call?" beats a Calendly link every time.

**Going silent after a positive reply.** We see this more than you'd think. A prospect says "sounds interesting, tell me more" and the client forgets to respond for a week. By then, the prospect has moved on.

**Over-pitching.** If someone asks a question, answer it. Don't redirect every message to a demo. The goal is a real conversation, not a funnel.
