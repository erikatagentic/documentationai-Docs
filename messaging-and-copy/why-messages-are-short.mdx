---
title: "Why Our Messages Are So Short"
---

If you looked at your messaging document and thought "that's it?", you're not alone. Here's why brevity is a strategy, not laziness.

## The Data

LinkedIn messages under 50 words consistently outperform longer messages. This isn't our opinion. It's what we've seen across hundreds of campaigns and what the data from 20M+ LinkedIn outreach attempts confirms.

The sweet spot for follow-up messages is 30-50 words. Anything over 75 words and reply rates start dropping. Connection notes (when we use them at all) are 15 words max.

## Why Short Works

**People skim LinkedIn messages.** They're checking on their phone between meetings. Your message gets 3 seconds of attention before they decide to reply or scroll past. A short, specific message earns a response. A wall of text earns a "not now."

**Short messages feel personal.** A 3-sentence DM looks like something a real person typed. A 3-paragraph pitch looks like marketing automation. Prospects can tell the difference.

**It forces clarity.** When you only have 40 words, every word has to earn its place. No filler, no corporate padding, no throat-clearing. The result is a message that respects the prospect's time and gets to the point.

## What About More Complex Offers?

If your service is technical or needs explanation, we don't try to explain it in the LinkedIn message. The message's job is to start a conversation, not close a deal. We lead with a relevant hook, ask a question, and save the details for when they reply.

## The Exception

InMail messages are slightly longer (50-65 words) because they go to non-connections who have no context on who you are. Even then, we keep them tighter than most people expect.

## Trust the Process

If you're worried the messages are too short, let the data speak. After 2-3 weeks of campaign data, we'll know exactly which messages are performing and can adjust from there. In our experience, "too short" is almost never the problem. "Too long" is.
