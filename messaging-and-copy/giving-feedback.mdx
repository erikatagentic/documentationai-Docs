---
title: "How to Give Feedback on Messaging"
---

Your input makes the messaging better. Here's how to give feedback that actually moves things forward.

## What Helps

**Correct the facts.** If we used the wrong stat, named the wrong service, or described your process incorrectly, tell us the right version. This is the most valuable feedback you can give.

**Tell us what your buyers care about.** "Our clients don't care about cost savings, they care about speed" is incredibly useful. We can shift the entire angle based on one insight like that.

**Flag tone mismatches.** If the messaging feels too formal, too casual, or doesn't match how your industry talks, say so. "My audience would never say 'scale their pipeline,' they'd say 'get more leads'" helps us dial in the voice.

**Share what prospects actually say on calls.** The objections, questions, and phrases your buyers use in real conversations are gold for messaging. If you hear "we already tried outbound and it didn't work" on 4 out of 5 calls, we should address that in the sequence.

## What Slows Things Down

**"I don't like it" without specifics.** We want to fix it, but we need to know what's off. Is it the tone? The angle? A factual error? The more specific, the faster we can turn it around.

**"Make it longer / add more about our services."** Longer messages consistently underperform on LinkedIn. We've tested this across hundreds of campaigns. If you want to include more detail, we'll find a way to work it in without killing the response rate.

**"Make it more professional."** This usually means making it sound more corporate, which is exactly what gets ignored on LinkedIn. If something feels too casual, give us a specific example and we'll find the right balance.

**Rewriting the entire message.** If you want to draft your own version, we're happy to test it as a variant. But our sequences are built on patterns we've tested across 50+ campaigns. Give the data a chance before overhauling.

## Turnaround

We usually turn around revisions within 24-48 hours. If we need more than one round of edits, we'll suggest a quick call to get aligned faster.
