---
title: "LinkedIn Account Disconnected"
---

A disconnection is different from a restriction. It means the platform lost its connection to your LinkedIn account. No outreach goes out while it's disconnected, but it's usually a quick fix.

## Common Causes

- **You changed your LinkedIn password.** This invalidates the existing session.
- **You updated your 2FA settings.** Adding, removing, or changing two-factor authentication resets the connection.
- **LinkedIn session expired.** Sessions occasionally expire on their own, especially after extended periods.
- **You logged in from an unusual location or device.** LinkedIn may force a re-authentication.

## What Happens Automatically

We monitor all sender accounts daily. When we detect a disconnection:
1. Campaigns on that account pause automatically. No messages go out.
2. We reconnect the account (this usually takes minutes, not hours).
3. If LinkedIn asks for a verification step (like confirming your identity), we'll reach out to you.

Most disconnections are resolved the same day, often before you even notice.

## What You Might Need to Do

In some cases, LinkedIn requires you to approve the reconnection. This could mean:
- Confirming a login notification in your LinkedIn app
- Entering a verification code sent to your email or phone
- Logging into LinkedIn directly and confirming the session

If we reach out about a disconnection, responding quickly gets your campaigns back online faster.

## How to Avoid Disconnections

You don't need to avoid using your LinkedIn account normally. Post content, accept connections, send messages. That's all fine. Just give your account manager a heads up before you:
- Change your LinkedIn password
- Update your 2FA settings
- Travel internationally (unusual login locations can trigger re-authentication)

A quick "heads up, changing my password today" lets us prepare and reconnect faster.

## Disconnected vs. Restricted

A disconnection is a technical issue (lost session). A restriction is LinkedIn limiting your account's activity. Disconnections are resolved by reconnecting. Restrictions require waiting for LinkedIn to lift them. See "My LinkedIn Account Got Restricted" for details on restrictions.
