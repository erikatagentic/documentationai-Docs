---
title: "My LinkedIn Account Got Restricted"
---

LinkedIn occasionally restricts accounts for unusual activity. If this happens to your sender profile, here's what it means and what we do about it.

## What a Restriction Looks Like

You might see a warning on LinkedIn when you log in, or your connection requests might stop going through. Sometimes LinkedIn restricts an account without any visible warning, and we catch it through our daily monitoring.

Common triggers:
- Sending too many connection requests in a short period
- A spike in profile views from automation
- Multiple login locations in a short window
- LinkedIn algorithm flagging patterns that look automated

## What We Do Immediately

As soon as we detect a restriction:

1. **We pause all campaigns** on that sender account. No more outreach goes out.
2. **We reduce daily limits** so when the account is back, it starts slow.
3. **We notify you** via email with what happened and what we recommend.
4. **We wait.** Most restrictions lift within 24-72 hours on their own.

## What You Need to Do

In most cases, nothing. LinkedIn restrictions typically resolve themselves. Avoid doing anything unusual on the account while it's restricted (don't send a bunch of manual connection requests or change your password repeatedly).

If LinkedIn asks you to verify your identity (phone number, email, or photo), do it promptly. That usually clears the restriction within hours.

## After the Restriction Lifts

We don't just flip everything back on. We ramp sending back up gradually over 3-5 days, starting at 50% of your previous daily volume. This avoids triggering another restriction.

## How We Prevent This

We run every sender account through a warmup period before launching campaigns. Daily limits start low (15-20 connection requests) and increase slowly. We also use dedicated residential proxies for each account, which prevents the "multiple login locations" trigger.

## When to Escalate

Most restrictions are temporary and harmless. But if your account gets suspended (not just restricted) or if LinkedIn asks you to verify with a government ID, let your account manager know immediately. That's a different situation that needs direct attention.

We monitor account health daily, so in most cases we catch issues before you do.
