---
title: "Low Reply Rate? Here's Why"
---

If your reply rate is below 10% and you've been running for 30+ days, the messaging is usually the issue. Here's what we look at.

## 1. Messaging Is Too Generic

The number one killer. If your follow-up messages could work for any company in any industry, they're not specific enough. Prospects can smell template outreach. Messages need to reference something real about their world, not just their job title.

**Fix:** We rewrite with a sharper angle. Instead of "Are you looking to grow your team?", something like "Saw you're hiring 3 engineers right now. Most companies your size hit a wall at hire 50. Worth a conversation?"

## 2. Messages Are Too Long

LinkedIn DMs over 75 words consistently underperform shorter ones. If your follow-up reads like a pitch deck, it's getting skipped.

**Fix:** We cut to 30-50 words. One idea per message, one question at the end. That's it.

## 3. Follow-Up Timing Is Off

Too aggressive (messaging every day) feels spammy. Too slow (waiting 2 weeks between messages) lets momentum die. The sweet spot is 2-7 days between follow-ups.

**Fix:** We adjust spacing. Most sequences work best with Follow-Up 1 at 1-2 days, Follow-Up 2 at 5-7 days, and Follow-Up 3 at 5-7 days after that.

## 4. Wrong Angle for the Audience

Sometimes the targeting is right but the messaging angle doesn't match what that audience cares about. You might be leading with cost savings when your ICP cares about speed. Or leading with features when they care about outcomes.

**Fix:** We revisit your case studies and customer feedback to find the angle that resonates. Then we A/B test it against the current copy.

## 5. Connection Rate Is Good, Reply Rate Is Low

This specific pattern (high acceptance, low replies) tells us the right people are connecting but the follow-up isn't compelling enough. The targeting is fine. The messaging needs work.

**Fix:** We focus entirely on the follow-up sequence. New hooks, different CTA, shorter messages, or a different value proposition in the opening line.

## The Typical Fix Timeline

Messaging changes usually show results within 7-14 days. We need enough volume (50+ messages sent with the new copy) to judge whether the change worked. Your account manager will track this and report back in your weekly update.
