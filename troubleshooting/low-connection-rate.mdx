---
title: "Low Connection Rate? Here's Why"
---

If your connection rate is below 15%, something needs adjusting. Here are the most common causes, in the order we check them.

## 1. Targeting Is Too Broad

The most common issue. If your lead list includes people who have no obvious reason to connect with you, accept rates drop. Tightening the ICP to a more specific set of titles, industries, or company sizes almost always helps.

**Fix:** We review your targeting criteria and narrow it. Sometimes the change is small, like going from "VP of Marketing" at all companies to "VP of Marketing" at B2B SaaS companies with 50-200 employees.

## 2. Your LinkedIn Profile Needs Work

Prospects check your profile before accepting. If your headline says "Sales Manager at [Company]" instead of something that signals value to them, they'll skip the request. A weak profile photo, sparse summary, or missing experience also hurts.

**Fix:** Depending on your package, profile optimization may be included. We'll present 3 options for your headline and summary. Ask your account manager if you're not sure what's covered.

## 3. Your Connection Note Is Hurting You

Counterintuitive, but adding a connection note often lowers accept rates compared to sending a blank request. Especially if the note sounds like a pitch. Notes that work are short (under 15 words) and give a reason to connect that's about them, not you.

**Fix:** We test removing the note entirely. In many cases, accept rates jump 10-20% when we switch to blank requests.

## 4. LinkedIn Account Health

If your sender account has been flagged by LinkedIn for unusual activity, connection requests may be getting throttled silently. You won't see an error, but fewer requests will actually reach prospects.

**Fix:** We monitor account health daily. If we spot throttling, we reduce daily limits, let the account cool down, and ramp back up gradually.

## 5. Sending Too Many Per Day

LinkedIn has informal limits on daily connection requests. Going above 30-40 per day on a newer or smaller account can trigger restrictions.

**Fix:** We set conservative limits during warmup (15-20/day) and increase gradually over 2-3 weeks. If you're seeing low accept rates and high volume, we'll dial it back.

## What Happens Next

If your connection rate is low, we don't just tweak one thing and hope. We test systematically: change one variable, run it for a week, compare. Your weekly update will show what we tested and what the results were.
